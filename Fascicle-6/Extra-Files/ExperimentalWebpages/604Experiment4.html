Poem 4 (J 127: 1830-1886)

df6 Houses— So the Wise Men tell me —
CP Houses— so the Wise Men tell me
bm Houses, so the wise men tell me ,
df6 Mansions ! Mansions must be warm !
CP Mansions ! Mansions must be warm !
bm Mansions ! Mansions must be warm ,
df6 Mansions cannot let the tears in,
CP Mansions cannot let the tears in,
bm Mansions cannot let the tears in,
df6 Mansions must Exclude the storm !
CP Mansions must exclude the storm !
bm Mansions must exclude the storm .
df6 Many Mansions, by his Father,
CP Many Mansions, by his Father,
bm Many mansions by His Father—
df6 I dont know him ; snugly built !
CP I don't know him ; snugly built !
bm I don't know him — snugly built .
df6 Could the children find the way there —
CP Could the Children find the way there —
bm Could the children find the way there
df6 Some , would Even trudge tonight!
CP Some , would even trudge tonight!
bm Some would even trudge tonight!
