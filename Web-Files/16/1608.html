Poem 8 (J 242: 1861/1945)

1:
When we stand on the tops of Things—
When we stand on the tops of things
2:
And like the Trees, look down—
And like the trees look down,
3:
The smoke all cleared away from it—
The smoke all cleared away from it
4:
And Mirrors on the scene—
And [sunset] on the scene
5:
Just laying light—no soul will wink
Just laying light, no soul will wink
6:
Except it have the flaw—
Except it have the flaw;
7:
The Sound ones, like the Hills VAR 1: —shall stand—VAR 2: —stand up—
The sound ones like the hills shall stand
8:
No Lightning, VAR 1: scares away—VAR 2: drives away—
No lightning drives away.
9:
The Perfect, nowhere be afraid—
The perfect nowhere be afraid,
10:
They bear their
They bear their
11:
VAR 1: dauntless Heads,VAR 2: fearless—Heads,VAR 3: tranquil—Heads,
tranquil heads
12:
When others, dare not VAR 1: go at Noon,VAR 2: walk at noon,
When others dare not go at Noon,go at noon,
13:
Protected by their deeds—
Protected by their deeds.
14:
The Stars dare shine occasionally
The stars dare shine occasionally
15:
Upon a spotted World—
Upon a spotted world,
16:
And Suns, go surer, for their Proof,
And suns go surer for their proof,
17:
As if VAR 1: An Axle, held—VAR 2: A Muscle—held
As if an axle held.
